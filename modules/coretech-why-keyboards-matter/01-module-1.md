## Content

### What is a virtual keyboard?

A virtual keyboard is software. When you press a key, the software changes what appears on screen — instead of the letter on your keyboard, you get the letter the language needs.

That's it. It's a layer between your physical keys and the screen. Keyman is the tool we use, and it supports more than 2,500 languages. It's free, it works on Windows (and other systems), and it's trusted by language communities and consultants around the world.

### Why does this matter for your work?

As a Language Technology Consultant, you often work with communities whose languages aren't supported by default. Without the right keyboard:

- Text can come out wrong, even when someone types carefully
- Symbols and special characters go missing
- Errors creep into Scripture translations, literacy materials, and data
- People give up and just use the wrong script — which causes more problems later

Knowing how to set up a virtual keyboard is one of the most practical things you can do to help.

### A quick note about Keyman

Keyman was developed by SIL International. It's free, open-source, and actively supported. This isn't a workaround or a temporary fix — it's a proper tool, used by serious language work around the world.

**TIP** You can find keyboards for hundreds of languages at [keyman.com/en/keyboards](https://keyman.com/en/keyboards/). It's worth exploring even before you need to install anything.
