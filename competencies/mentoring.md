# Mentoring

**Category:** Core

## Sub-competencies

### Attitudes

Creates a positive environment that encourages mentees to grow professionally.

**Observable criteria**

- Shows genuine care, celebrates successes, and recognizes personal limits.
- Believes in the mentee’s potential and highlights their achievements.

**Why it matters**

- Attitudes of listening and encouragement are key to establishing trust and motivating mentees.

### Principles

Understands the difference between formal mentoring, informal mentoring, coaching, and facilitation.

**Observable criteria**

- Explains how formal mentoring works (objectives, roles, duration).
- Clarifies mutual expectations (mentor, mentee, supervisor) at the start.

**Why it matters**

- A clear framework (objectives, boundaries, follow-up) ensures mentoring is effective and that each person knows their role.

### Skills

Uses the necessary know-how to guide and support mentees.

**Observable criteria**

- Sets specific objectives with the mentee and follows up regularly.
- Provides respectful and constructive feedback.
- Maintains confidentiality regarding shared experiences.

**Why it matters**

- Mentoring strengthens everyone’s ability to learn, to train others, and to contribute to the overall development of the team.
