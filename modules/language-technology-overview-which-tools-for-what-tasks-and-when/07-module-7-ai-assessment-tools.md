# Module 7: AI & Assessment Tools

## Connect (5 minutes)
**Think about this:** Technology is changing rapidly. AI tools are now helping with quality assessment, audio processing, and even generating translations into hundreds of languages.
How can you stay current while also ensuring teams use AI tools wisely and ethically?
### Content (25 minutes)
Play the video AI and Assessment Tools
**AI Tools for Language Work**
**AQuA (Augmented Quality Assessment)** - Translation quality copilot
- Developed by SIL International
- Develops capacity and increases thoroughness of quality assurance
- Gives the big picture on translation quality
- Helps teams identify issues systematically
**AERO** - Audio processing tools
- Various audio processing capabilities
- Rapidly expanding scope
- Addresses audio quality and processing needs
**Alpha2** - Non-scripture translation and audio generation
- Translates non-scripture materials into hundreds of languages
- Text-to-Speech (TTS) generation
- Helps identify problems instantly through audio output
**Faithbridge** - Multilingual chatbot
- Access to Bible translation resources
- AI-powered resource discovery and assistance
**Language Assessment Tools**
**Cog** - Modern language comparison
- Compares languages using statistics and comparative linguistics
- Automates word list comparison from different language varieties
- Helps determine language relationships and distinctions
**WordSurv** - Legacy assessment tool
- Older tool for language assessment tasks
- Being replaced by more modern solutions like Cog
**Purpose:** Determine if communities speak the same language or need separate translations
### Challenge (25 minutes)
**Scenario-Based Problem Solving:**
**Scenario 1 - AI Ethics:** A team wants to use Alpha2 to generate initial translations of literacy materials into their language. They ask, “Can AI just do the translation work for us?”
How do you respond? What’s appropriate AI use vs. inappropriate?
**Scenario 2 - Language Assessment:** Two villages 30km apart each claim they speak different languages and both want Bible translation projects. A donor asks you to determine if they really need two separate translations.
What tool would you use? What kind of data would you need?
**Scenario 3 - Quality Assessment:** A translation team has completed the Gospel of John but wants to ensure quality before consultant checking. They’ve heard about AQuA but aren’t sure how to use it.
What would you tell them about AQuA’s purpose and benefits?
### Change (10 minutes)
**Self-Assessment:**
- How do AI tools enhance (not replace) human translation work?
- What’s the purpose of language assessment, and why does it matter for translation projects?
**Critical Thinking:**
- As AI tools rapidly expand, what principles should guide their ethical use in Bible translation?
- How do you balance using new technology with maintaining translation quality and community ownership?
