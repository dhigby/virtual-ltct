## 1. Visual Enhancements
To help experienced translators quickly grasp the scale of what they are converting, consider adding a visual reference section.
## 2. Structural Suggestions
### **A. Refine the "Time of Day" Section**
In Step 4, under "Numbers and Numerical Expressions," you mention "The third hour." For an experienced translator, this is a major pain point.
- **Suggestion:** Explicitly mention the difference between the **Jewish/Roman timekeeping** (counting from sunrise) vs. **Modern timekeeping**.
- **Action:** Add a small table or bullet point:
	- *The Third Hour:* 9:00 AM
	- *The Sixth Hour:* 12:00 PM (Noon)
	- *The Ninth Hour:* 3:00 PM
### **B. Expand on "Money" (Inflation & Value)**
The value of money is the most volatile element in translation.
- **Suggestion:** Add a "Warning" note about using specific modern currency (e.g., USD, Euros, or local Pesos).
- **Reasoning:** Inflation can make a translation feel "dated" within only 5–10 years.
- **Better Practice:** Suggest using "days of wages" as the functional equivalent in text, as it scales with time and culture better than a specific number.
### **C. The "Cultural Clash" Check**
- **Suggestion:** Add a specific check for **Idiomatic Numbers**.
- **Example:** In some cultures, "40" isn't just a number; it's an idiom for "a long time." An experienced translator needs to decide if they translate the literal number \$40\$ or the "feeling" of a generation.

## 4. Suggested Content Edits (Textual)
**In Step 4 (Option 4: Combination Approach):**
> **Current:** `ten talents^a^` with footnote `^a^ About 340 kg of silver...`
	**Improvement:** Suggest including the **ratio** in the footnote.
	*Example:* `^a^ Ten talents was a massive sum, equivalent to about 340 kg of silver or 200 years of a laborer’s wages.` (Using "years of wages" often hits harder for the reader than "kilograms").
**In the Troubleshooting Section:**
- **Add a Problem:** "The measurement is used metaphorically (e.g., 'A Sabbath day's journey' used to describe a short distance, not a literal travel limit)."
- **Solution:** Advise translators to prioritize the **metaphorical impact** over the literal conversion in poetic or hyperbolic contexts.

## 5. Formatting for Scannability
The module is long (50 minutes). To keep an "Experienced Translator" engaged, use more **Bold Callouts** for the "Why" behind the "How."
> **💡 Pro Tip: The "Mental Load" Test**
