Proper names and biblical terms are the backbone of Scripture translation. Inconsistency in these foundational elements undermines reader confidence and creates confusion. Even one missing or inconsistent rendering can be noticed by readers and create questions about the translation's reliability.
This module guides you through the final systematic review of all proper names and major biblical terms to ensure nothing has been missed and everything is consistent.

## Procedure Walkthrough
**Time:** 40 minutes
### Understanding Biblical Terms and Proper Names
The Biblical Terms tool in Paratext tracks important words and concepts throughout Scripture, ensuring they're translated consistently. This includes:
**Proper names:**
- People (Moses, David, Paul, Mary)
- Places (Jerusalem, Galilee, Rome, Egypt)
- Groups (Pharisees, Sadducees, Gentiles)
**Key theological terms:**
- God, Lord, Holy Spirit
- Sin, redemption, salvation
- Covenant, law, grace
**Important concepts:**
- Kingdom of God/heaven
- Son of Man, Son of God
- Righteousness, faith
The **Major Biblical Terms list** contains the most important terms that appear frequently and carry significant theological weight. Before publication, every term on this list should have an approved rendering.
### Why This Final Check Matters
You've been working with biblical terms throughout the translation process, but this final check ensures:
1. **Nothing was missed** - Easy to overlook a name that appears only a few times
2. **Recent changes are reflected** - Late revisions may have introduced gaps
3. **Consistency is verified** - Even approved renderings should be checked across contexts
4. **Team agreement is confirmed** - Final sign-off before publication
**Key principle:** Missing renderings discovered after publication require expensive reprinting or leave readers with untranslated words.
> <span discussion-urls="discussion://2f7598a5-fd40-805d-8b01-fc366a965214/2f7598a5-fd40-8079-b736-c85c29b1498e/37a598a5-fd40-8094-b2ed-001c9b1d8b8e">⚠️ Important: One missing name can confuse readers for the entire New Testament. A reader encounters "Παῦλος" untranslated because you forgot to add a rendering? They'll wonder if it's the same person as "Paul" or someone different. Don't let this happen!</span>

### Step 1: Open the Biblical Terms Tool
1. Click in your project window to make it active
2. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Biblical Terms **
	- The Biblical Terms window opens
	- You'll see a list of terms with their renderings
### Step 2: Select the Major Biblical Terms List
Not all biblical terms are equally important. The Major Biblical Terms list focuses on the most crucial ones for publication.
1. In the Biblical Terms window: **≡ Tab** \> **Biblical Terms** \> **Select Biblical Terms List**
2. Choose **Major Biblical Terms** from the list
3. Click **OK**
**What you'll see:**
- The window now shows only major biblical terms
- This is a more manageable list than "All Biblical Terms"
- These are the terms that absolutely must have renderings before publication
### Step 3: Filter for Names with Missing Renderings
You want to focus on terms that still need work.
1. Look for the filter options in the Biblical Terms window
	- Usually at the top of the window or in a filter menu
2. Select the filter for **Missing Renderings** or **Terms without renderings**
	- Different Paratext versions may have slightly different wording
3. Apply the filter
**What you'll see:**
- Only terms missing renderings are displayed
- The list should be much shorter than the full Major Biblical Terms list
- Each term shows where it appears in Scripture but has no rendering assigned
**If the list is empty:**
- Excellent! All major biblical terms have renderings
- You can skip to Step 5 to verify consistency
- But first double-check: *Verify that your 'Search' field is empty. Sometimes a stray character in the search box can make the list appear empty when it isn't.*
> 💡 Tip: If your filtered list is empty, congratulations! But don't skip the verification step (Step 5). Even with all renderings present, you need to check for consistency and appropriateness across contexts.
**If the list has many items:**
- Don't panic—this is normal if it's your first comprehensive review
- Work through them systematically
- Some may be intentional (see Step 4)
### Step 4: Review and Add Renderings
Now work through each term that needs a rendering.
### For Each Term Without a Rendering:
1. **Click on the term** to see all its occurrences
	- The lower panel shows every place this term appears
	- Read the contexts to understand how it's used
2. **Check if a rendering already exists in the text**
	- Sometimes translators add renderings directly in the text without recording them in the Biblical Terms tool
	- If you see a consistent translation already in use, record it
3. **Determine the appropriate rendering**
	**For proper names (people and places):**
	- Follow your project's transliteration guidelines
	- Check how related languages handle the name
	- Ensure consistency with how you've handled similar names
	- Consider ease of pronunciation for your language speakers
	**For theological terms:**
	- Review how the term has been translated in context
	- Ensure consistency with your translation philosophy
	- Consult your Translation Consultant for difficult terms
	- Consider established terminology in your language community
4. **Add the rendering**
	- Click in the rendering field
	- Type your chosen rendering
	- Save (the tool usually auto-saves)
5. **Add a note if needed**
	- Document why this rendering was chosen
	- Note any team discussion or consultant input
	- Reference related decisions
	<callout icon="💡" color="green_bg">
		**Pro-Tip:** Adding a rendering to the list tells Paratext what to *look* for. It does not automatically change the words in your verses. After adding a rendering, use the **Renderings** pane to click the **blue arrow** or use **Ctrl+R** to apply the rendering to the verse.
	</callout>
### Special Cases
**Names that appear only once or twice:**
- Still need renderings for completeness
- May require less discussion than frequently-used names
- Check published resources for standard renderings
**Names with variant forms:**
- Example: "Simon" vs. "Simon Peter" vs. "Peter"
- These may be separate entries or linked entries
- Ensure all variants are consistent
**Terms your team discussed but decided not to translate:**
- Some terms might be left in transliterated form intentionally
- Still record the transliteration in the rendering field
- Add a note explaining the decision
**Terms that need Translation Consultant input:**
- Flag these for review
- Don't guess on difficult theological terms
- Document the question and wait for consultant response
> ❌ Common Mistake: Guessing at difficult theological terms because you "don't want to bother" the consultant. Your Translation Consultant WANTS to help with these decisions—that's their job! Don't rob them of the opportunity to contribute to quality.
### Step 5: Verify Consistency Across Contexts
Even if all terms have renderings, verify they're used consistently:
1. **Remove the "missing renderings" filter** to see all major biblical terms
2. **Scan through the list** looking for:
	- Terms with multiple renderings (may indicate inconsistency)
	- Recently added renderings (verify they're appropriate)
	- Complex terms (double-check they work in all contexts)
3. **Click on any questionable terms** to review all occurrences
	- Read several contexts
	- Verify the rendering works appropriately in each
	- Check that it's actually being used (not just recorded but not applied)
4. **Look for related terms** that should be consistent
	- Example: "kingdom of God" and "kingdom of heaven"
	- Example: "Lord" when referring to God vs. human masters
	- Example: "grace" as a greeting vs. theological concept
### Step 6: Final Team Review
Before considering this check complete:
1. **Export the biblical terms list** for team review
	- **≡ Tab** \> **Biblical Terms** \> **Export to HTML** or **Print**
	- Distribute to team members
2. **Schedule a team meeting** to review:
	- Any newly added renderings
	- Difficult terms that generated discussion
	- Terms where team members have concerns
3. **Document team decisions**
	- Record consensus in the Biblical Terms notes
	- Flag items for Translation Consultant review if needed
4. **Get Translation Consultant approval**
	- Share your biblical terms list with your consultant
	- Address any concerns they raise
	- Make revisions as needed

## Troubleshooting
**Time:** 5 minutes
### Common Issues and Solutions
**Problem:** "The filter for missing renderings shows nothing, but I know there are gaps"
- **Solution:** The filter may be set incorrectly. Try: (1) Clear all filters and manually look through the list, (2) Check if you're viewing the correct biblical terms list (Major vs. All), (3) Verify the filter is set to "missing" not "present" renderings.
**Problem:** "A term shows as having a rendering, but when I look at the actual text, it's inconsistent"
- **Solution:** The Biblical Terms tool records approved renderings but doesn't enforce their use. You need to: (1) Use the Biblical Terms tool to apply the rendering consistently throughout, (2) Run a Find & Replace to standardize usage, (3) Manually review each occurrence to ensure proper rendering.
**Problem:** "I'm not sure how to transliterate a particular name"
- **Solution:** Check: (1) Your project's transliteration guidelines, (2) How related languages handle the name, (3) Published resources in your language, (4) Ask your Translation Consultant for guidance. Don't guess—inconsistent name spellings are very noticeable.
**Problem:** "My team disagrees on the rendering for an important theological term"
- **Solution:** (1) Document both positions clearly, (2) Review the term in all its contexts together, (3) Consult published theological resources, (4) Ask your Translation Consultant for input and decision. Don't leave this unresolved—theological terms are too important.
**Problem:** "A term appears in one book but not others we're publishing. Does it need a rendering?"
- **Solution:** Yes. Even if a term appears only once in your publication, it needs a rendering. Readers may eventually have the full Bible, and consistency across all books matters.
**Problem:** "Some names have variant forms (like Simon/Peter). How do I handle these?"
- **Solution:** Each variant should have its own entry with its own rendering. Ensure the variants are related and consistent. For example, if you translate "Simon" one way, "Simon Peter" should include that same rendering of "Simon" plus your rendering of "Peter."
**Problem:** "The Biblical Terms tool is showing terms I know aren't in the books I'm publishing"
- **Solution:** Check which books are selected in your filter settings. The tool may be showing terms from all books in your project, not just those being published. Adjust the book filter to show only publication books.
**Problem:** "I added a rendering but it's not showing up in the text"
- **Solution:** Adding a rendering to the Biblical Terms tool doesn't automatically change your text. You need to: (1) Apply the rendering to each occurrence using the tool, OR (2) Use Find & Replace to update the text, OR (3) Manually edit each location.
### When to Ask for Help
**Contact your Language Technology Consultant if:**
- The Biblical Terms tool isn't working properly (technical issue)
- Filters aren't functioning as expected (software problem)
- You can't export or print the biblical terms list (system issue)
- The tool isn't recognizing renderings you've added (configuration problem)
**Contact your Translation Consultant if:**
- You need guidance on translating difficult theological terms (content question)
- Your team is divided on how to render an important concept (translation decision)
- You're unsure about transliteration conventions for names (linguistic issue)
- You need final approval before publication (quality verification)
