# Scholarship and Documentation

**Category:** Core · **Guide section:** Research and Documentation

## Sub-competencies

### Intellectual Property

Understands basic concepts of copyright and licensing for resources and technology tools.

**Observable criteria**

- Distinguishes between different license types (copyright, Creative Commons, etc.).
- Adheres to copyright regulations in all projects.

**Why it matters**

- Working ethically and legally in terms of intellectual property rights protects the project and encourages responsible resource sharing.

### Publication and Archiving

Knows how to document and archive resources so they remain accessible and reusable.

**Observable criteria**

- Writes training or reference materials that are clear and concise.
- Classifies and archives documents (text files, databases, etc.) in an organized manner.

**Why it matters**

- Good document management capitalizes on completed work and avoids duplication of effort in future projects.

### Research

Keeps abreast of new developments and contributes, at one’s own level, to improving practices or research in the field.

**Observable criteria**

- Stays informed about updates (software, methods, etc.).
- Shares experiences or observations with the community.
- Asks questions and actively participates in trainings, workshops, or conferences.

**Why it matters**

- Staying current and exchanging best practices fosters innovation and increases the quality of services provided.
