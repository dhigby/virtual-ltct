Quote marks are probably the hardest punctuation mark to get right and use consistently. They\$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$re difficult to get right partly because it\$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$s easy to forget to put them in where they\$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$re needed, partly because there are different levels of quotes, partly because there are different kinds of quotes, and partly because each language has their own way of handling what people say and how they say what someone else said. Are you confused yet? яБК
Needless to say, there are many different ways to handle quotes and the marking of them. Here are a few ways...
(1) Perhaps the most common way is to use **double** quote marks for quotes and **single** quote marks for quotes inside of quotes.<br>*They discussed it among themselves and said, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЬ\}\$ If we say, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАШ\}\$ From heaven, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$ he will ask, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАШ\}\$ Why didn\$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$t you believe him? \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$ But if we say, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАШ\}\$ Of human origin, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЩ\}\$ all the people will stone us, because they are persuaded that John was a prophet . \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЭ\}\$ тАУ Luke 20:\$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{5\}\$тАУ6*
A third quote level тАУ a quote inside a quote inside a quote тАУ is sometimes marked with a colon along with (or instead of) an opening quote mark in an effort to help make the change in speech a bit more clear.<br>*Then the LORD said to Moses, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЬ\}\$ Go to Pharaoh and say to him, \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАШ\}\$ This is what the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, says \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{: тАЬ\}\$ Let my people go, so that they may worship me. тАж \$\\color\{#0000ff\}\{тАЭ тАЩ тАЭ\}\$ тАУ Exodus 9:1*

(3) If quotes begin with a speech formula (i.e. a word or phrase that always appears at the beginning of a quote), then you could opt to use only closing quote marks, but not opening ones.
(4) You might not need to use quote marks at all, if the language uses a speech verb every time someone begins speaking and an affix and/or other marker that indicates when the person stops speaking.
(5) Another way to handle quotes is to mark different kinds of speech/quotes using different usfm codes in Paratext, which can then be formatted in different ways during typesetting.
