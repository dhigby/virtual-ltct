# Adult Education

**Category:** Core

## Sub-competencies

### Practice

Applies methods suited to training adults in diverse contexts.

**Observable criteria**

- Identifies the specific needs of adult learners (technical level, culture, etc.).
- Uses participatory, hands-on approaches to encourage active learning.
- Adapts teaching style to cultural differences.

**Why it matters**

- Training adults involves varying methods (demonstrations, hands-on exercises, discussions) and respecting each person’s learning pace.

### Theory

Knows the main principles of adult education and can explain them simply.

**Observable criteria**

- Explains the basics of active learning (dialogue, role-plays, hands-on activities, etc.).
- Recognizes that learners have diverse styles (auditory, visual, kinesthetic).

**Why it matters**

- A solid grasp of adult education theory enhances session design and delivery, leading to better outcomes and knowledge transfer.
