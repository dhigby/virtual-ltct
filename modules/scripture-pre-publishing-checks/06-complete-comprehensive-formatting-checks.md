## Introduction
**Time:** 5 minutes
Formatting errors are invisible during translation work—until they're printed in thousands of copies and it's too late to fix them.
> ⚠️ Important: Formatting errors can't be easily fixed after printing. A missing section heading, broken cross-reference, or incomplete footnote will be in every copy of your published Bible. This module is unglamorous but essential.
A missing section heading here, an inconsistent book title there, a footnote with broken reference formatting—these seem like minor issues during translation. But in a published Bible, they undermine professionalism and distract readers from the message.
Formatting checks are the unglamorous but essential final quality control before publication. This module guides you through Paratext's checklist tools to systematically verify that every formatting element is correct and consistent.
### What This Module Covers
- Reviewing formatting check module (FC) completion
- Checking verse lengths and divisions
- Verifying section headings
- Confirming book titles are correct
- Checking cross-references
- Reviewing footnote formatting
### Prerequisites
Before starting this module, ensure:
- You've previously completed the FC (Formatting Checks) module
- You're familiar with USFM formatting markers
- You have editing permissions for all books
- You understand your project's style guidelines for headings and references

### Understanding Formatting Checks
Formatting checks verify the structure and presentation elements of your text:
**What formatting includes:**
- Section headings that break up text
- Book titles and subtitles
- Verse numbers and divisions
- Cross-references to other passages
- Footnotes with explanatory content
- Formatting markers (USFM codes)
**Why formatting matters:**
- Readers use headings to navigate and find passages
- Book titles identify what they're reading
- References help readers explore related passages
- Footnotes provide essential explanations
- Formatting markers control how typesetters render the final publication
**The challenge:** You've been focused on translation quality. Formatting may have been neglected or become inconsistent through multiple revisions. 
Repeat each step until all errors are corrected.

### Step 2: Check Long and Short Verses
Verses that are unusually long or short may indicate formatting problems.
1. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Checklists** \> **Long/short verses**
**What this checks:**
- Verses significantly longer than typical
- Verses significantly shorter than typical
- May indicate missing or extra verse breaks
- May indicate content accidentally deleted or duplicated
**Review the results:**
For each flagged verse:
**If verse is flagged as too long:**
- Read the verse carefully
- Is there actually too much content?
- Did you accidentally include content from the next verse?
- Is there a missing verse break (\\v marker)?
- Or is the verse legitimately long in the original?
**If verse is flagged as too short:**
- Is content missing?
- Was material accidentally deleted?
- Is the verse actually short in the original?
- Did you inadvertently insert an extra verse break?
**Not all long/short verses are errors.** Some biblical verses are genuinely long or short. The checklist just flags unusual cases for review.
> 💡 Tip: If you see the same verse flagged repeatedly in multiple checks, that's a clue it might be a real issue. But if it's only flagged once and the content looks correct, it's probably fine.
**Action:** Correct actual errors, note intentional variations.
### Step 3: Check Section Headings
Section headings help readers navigate the text. This checklist verifies they're present and properly formatted.
1. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Checklists** \> **Section headings**
**What this checks:**
- All section headings are present
- Headings use correct USFM markers
- Heading style is consistent
- Headings appear at appropriate intervals
**Review the results:**
**For missing headings:**
- Identify long passages without headings
- Determine if headings are needed
- Add appropriate headings following your project style
**For formatting issues:**
- Verify heading markers (\\s, \\s1, \\s2) are used correctly
- Check capitalization is consistent
- Ensure style matches project guidelines
**For heading content:**
- Are headings clear and helpful?
- Do they accurately reflect the content?
- Are they appropriate for your target audience?
**Consistency matters:**
- Similar passage types should have similar heading styles
- Frequency of headings should be relatively consistent
- Language and tone should match throughout
### Step 4: Check Book Titles
Book titles must be correct and consistently formatted.
1. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Checklists** \> **Book titles**
**What this checks:**
- \\h (header) line is present in each book
- \\mt1 (main title) is present and correct
- \\mt2, \\mt3 (subtitles) are used appropriately if needed
- Titles match your project's approved book names
**Review each book:**
**Header line (\\h):**
- Should appear at the very beginning of each book
- Usually short form of book name
- Example: `\h Matthew` or `\h The Gospel of Matthew`
**Main title (\\mt1):**
- Should appear after \\h, before actual text
- Full book title as it will appear in publication
- Example: `\mt1 The Gospel According to Matthew`
**Subtitles (\\mt2, \\mt3):**
- Optional additional title information
- May include alternative names or descriptions
- Must use correct markers
- Example: `\mt2 The First Book`
**Verify:**
- All books have required title elements
- Spelling is exactly correct
- Formatting markers are proper
- Titles match approved book name list
### Step 5: Check Cross-References
Cross-references point readers to related passages. They must be accurate and properly formatted.
1. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Checklists** \> **References**
**What this checks:**
- Cross-reference markers are properly formatted
- References point to valid biblical locations
- Reference syntax is correct
- References are complete (not missing book/chapter/verse)
**Review the results:**
**For formatting errors:**
- Verify \\x ... \\x\* markers are used correctly
- Check reference format matches project style
- Ensure references are readable
**For accuracy:**
- Do references point to the correct passages?
- Are book abbreviations correct?
- Are chapter and verse numbers valid?
**For completeness:**
- Are all intended cross-references present?
- Are any references broken or incomplete?
**Common issues:**
- Typos in book abbreviations
- Invalid chapter or verse numbers
- Missing parts of references
- Inconsistent reference formatting
**Action:** Correct errors, verify accuracy, ensure consistency.
### Step 6: Check Footnotes
Footnotes provide explanations, alternative translations, and textual notes. They must be complete and properly formatted.
1. **≡ Tab** \> **Tools** \> **Checklists** \> **Footnotes**
**What this checks:**
- Footnote markers (\\f ... \\f\*) are properly formatted
- Footnote components are complete
- Footnotes aren't broken or incomplete
- Footnote formatting is consistent
**Review each footnote:**
**Structure elements:**
- \\f begins the footnote
- \\fr contains the verse reference
- \\ft contains the footnote text
- \\f\* ends the footnote
**Verify:**
- All footnotes have all required components
- Verse references in \\fr are correct
- Footnote text in \\ft is clear and helpful
- Closing marker \\f\* is present
- No broken or incomplete footnotes
**Content review:**
- Is the footnote content accurate?
- Is it helpful for readers?
- Is it necessary, or could it be removed?
- Does it match your project's footnote guidelines?
**Consistency:**
- Similar situations use similar footnote styles
- Language and tone are consistent
- Formatting is uniform throughout

## Troubleshooting
**Time:** 3 minutes
### Common Issues and Solutions
**Problem:** "Long/short verses checklist flags many verses, but they're correct"
- **Solution:** Biblical verses vary in length naturally. Review flagged verses, but if they're accurate to the original, note them as correct. The checklist just identifies unusual cases for review.
**Problem:** "I can't find a specific checklist mentioned in this module"
- **Solution:** Checklist availability varies by Paratext version. If a specific checklist isn't available, ask your Language Technology Consultant for alternatives or updates.
**Problem:** "Section headings checklist shows I'm missing headings, but I intentionally didn't add them"
- **Solution:** Some projects use fewer headings than others. If your project style uses minimal headings, document this decision. Ensure the choice is intentional and approved by your Translation Consultant.
**Problem:** "Book titles are flagged as incorrect, but they match our approved list"
- **Solution:** The checklist may be comparing to a different standard. Verify your titles match your project's official book name list. If there's a discrepancy, consult your Language Technology Consultant about updating the checking configuration.
**Problem:** "Cross-references have errors, but I don't know what the correct references should be"
- **Solution:** Cross-references should be based on scholarly resources or reference Bibles. Don't guess. Check: (1) Your source text's cross-references, (2) Published reference Bibles, (3) Ask your Translation Consultant for guidance.
**Problem:** "Footnotes are inconsistent because different translators wrote them"
- **Solution:** Standardize footnote style across the project. Create a footnote style guide, then work through systematically to make them consistent. This may take several hours but is essential for professional quality.
**Problem:** "I fixed errors but the checklist still shows them"
- **Solution:** After making corrections: (1) Save your changes, (2) Close and reopen the checklist, (3) Or run the checklist again. Checklists don't auto-update as you make changes.
### When to Ask for Help
**Contact your Language Technology Consultant if:**
- Checklist tools aren't working properly (technical issue)
- You need help understanding what a checklist is checking (tool function)
- Checklists are giving false positives (configuration problem)
- You can't resolve formatting marker issues (USFM question)
**Contact your Translation Consultant if:**
- You're unsure what content should be in footnotes (translation decision)
- You need guidance on section heading style (publication standards)
- Cross-reference accuracy questions (biblical scholarship)
- Book title formatting questions (publication conventions)

### Key Takeaways
- Formatting checks use systematic checklists to verify publication readiness
- Previous FC module work should be reviewed and issues resolved
- Five key checklists cover verse length, headings, titles, references, and footnotes
- Not all flagged items are errors—some require judgment about appropriateness
- Consistency in formatting creates professional, reader-friendly publications
- Documentation of decisions helps with future work and team continuity
### What Comes Next
After completing formatting checks, the final module covers **Glossary Linking**. You'll learn to:
- Mark glossary words in the text with links

### Timeline Guidance
Formatting checks typically take:
- **Running initial checklists:** 1-2 hours
- **Reviewing flagged items:** 3-6 hours (depending on number of issues)
- **Correcting errors:** 2-8 hours (varies widely by project condition)
- **Final verification:** 1-2 hours
**Budget extra time if:**
- This is your first comprehensive formatting review
- Multiple translators worked on the project (more inconsistency)
- You haven't completed the FC module previously
- You're standardizing footnote or heading styles across books
**Plan for iterative work:**
- Run checklist
- Fix a batch of issues
- Run checklist again
- Continue until clean
> ⏱️ Time Saver: Don't try to fix everything in one sitting. Work in batches: fix 10-20 items, take a break, then fix the next batch. This prevents fatigue-induced errors and makes the work feel less overwhelming.
