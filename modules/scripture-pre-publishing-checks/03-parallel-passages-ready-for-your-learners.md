## Module 3: Comprehensive Checking Workflows: Parallel Passages and Verification
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Level:** Experienced Translator
**Goal:** Verify parallel passages are consistent and confirm all required checks have been completed before publication
### Introduction
**Time:** 5 minutes
You think you're finished. The translation is done, consultants have approved it, illustrations are added, back matter is prepared. But have you checked **everything**?
One of the most embarrassing discoveries after publication is finding inconsistencies between parallel passages—the same story told differently in different Gospels, but translated with different vocabulary in your language. Or discovering you missed an entire category of checks and there are still formatting errors throughout.
This module guides you through the systematic verification process to ensure your translation is truly ready for publication. You'll check parallel passages for consistency and run comprehensive verification to confirm nothing has been missed.

### Procedure Walkthrough
**Time:** 40 minutes
### Part 1: Check Parallel Passages
Parallel passages are portions of Scripture that appear in multiple books, often with similar or identical wording.
**Why this matters:** Readers notice when the same event is described using different vocabulary. While some variation is acceptable (different Gospel writers emphasized different details), major inconsistencies can confuse readers or make them question the translation's accuracy.
> ⚠️ **Important:** The goal is **NOT** to make all parallel passages word-for-word identical. The goal is consistency in key vocabulary while respecting the original writer's intentions.
**Using the Parallel Passages Module**
> 💡 **Tip: Using Filters to Save Time**
	In the Parallel Passages tool, use the dropdown menu (which defaults to **All references**) to narrow your focus:
	- **Unapproved references:** This is your "To-Do" list. It shows only the passages you haven't marked as approved yet.
	- **Changed text:** Shows only passages edited since the last time you checked.
**Basic process:**
1. **Open the tool:** `≡ Tab > Tools > Parallel Passages`.
2. **Check the Source Language Row:** Before harmonizing, look at the Hebrew/Greek source row. If necessary, click the **v** in front of the Heb/Grk line to show the text. If the source text is different, your translation should likely remain different.
3. **Use a Comparative Text:** Click the **Comparative Text** button on the toolbar to select a reference project. Use this to see how other translations handled the parallel.
4. **Review for consistency:** Are key terms translated the same way? Are there appropriate reasons for differences?
5. **Mark as Approved:** Click the checkbox to the left of the passage. Once approved, it will disappear from the **Unapproved references** list.
### Part 2: Verify All Checks Are Complete
**Method: Multiple Books (Comprehensive Approach)**
1. **Run Basic Checks:** `≡ Tab > Tools > Run Basic Checks`.
2. **Select All:** Ensure **ALL** check types and **ALL** books to be published are selected (including Front Matter, Glossary, etc.).
	- *Note: Be prepared for unique errors in non-scripture books where rules may differ.*
3. **Review Results:** \> 💡 **Tip:** Rather than simply "ignoring" issues in the checklist, mark them as **Valid** in the corresponding **Inventory**. This ensures the "error" is cleared for the whole team and won't reappear after a Send/Receive.
4. **Update Inventories:** If you find many errors (e.g., punctuation), go to `≡ Tab > Tools > Checking inventories`.
	- Mark unknown items as **Valid** (acceptable) or **Invalid** (error).
	- **Sync Your Results:** You **must** Send/Receive after updating inventories so your team sees the same results.
### Part 3: Word List Checks
**Check 1: Spell Check**
> 💡 **Pro-Tip for Proper Names:** Before running Spell Check, open your Biblical Terms list and go to **Tools \> Mark renderings as correctly spelled...** This "pushes" your approved names into the dictionary so they aren't flagged as errors.
1. **Run Check:** `≡ Tab > Tools > Spell Check > All Checks`.
2. **Decide:** For flagged words, check if it's a Proper Name in your Biblical Terms renderings. If not, Correct, Add to dictionary, or Ignore.
**Check 2: Find Similar Words**
- `≡ Tab > Tools > Find Similar Words`.
- Identifies inconsistent spelling (e.g., "Jerusalem" vs "Jerusalam"). Essential for proper names.

- **Paper Review:** \> 💡 **Tip:** Use `File > Print` to export as HTML from the Parallel Passages window, or `File > Export as draft PDF` from the main window. It is often easier to spot rhythm and flow inconsistencies on paper than on a screen.
