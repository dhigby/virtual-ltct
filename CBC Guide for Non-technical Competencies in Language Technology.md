# CBC Guide for Non-technical Competencies in Language Technology

This **simplified version** focuses on the essentials for **Language Technology (LT) Consultants**, emphasizing:

- **Technical problem-solving**  
- **Interpersonal and intercultural competencies**  
- **Adult education** and **mentoring**  
- **Data organization, communication, and management**

Each competency includes a **description**, **components** (observable criteria), and **instructions** (relevance and rationale). These elements can serve as a basis for **evaluating progress** and **developing** each LT consultant’s skill set, while remaining accessible to people with varying academic levels.

---

# Professional Competencies

## Participation in the Community of Practice (CoP)

**Competency Description**  
Can communicate with peers (other technicians, developers, trainers) to ask questions, share solutions, and collaborate.

**Components**

- Participates in discussions on appropriate platforms (forums, email, messaging groups, etc.).  
- Regularly shares discoveries or best practices.  
- Seeks help and offers support to other members.

**Instructions**

- Mutual help and knowledge exchange with peers strengthen skills and improve the quality of support provided to field teams.

---

## English Language

**Competency Description**  
Is able to understand technical documentation in English and pass on key information to those who do not read English.

**Components**

- Reads and understands instructions, manuals, and tutorials in English.  
- Communicates key points orally or in writing in the local language.

**Instructions**

- Many training resources, tools, and LT (Language Technology) documentation are only available in English; having English proficiency facilitates learning and sharing.

---

## Problem-Solving

**Competency Description**  
Identifies and resolves technical issues effectively and quickly.

**Components**

- Quickly analyzes the nature of the issue (technical, software, hardware, etc.).  
- Compares different possible solutions.  
- Implements the best solution and verifies its effectiveness.

**Instructions**

- LT consultants often face a wide variety of technical problems under limited resources; efficient problem-solving is essential.

---

## Responsive Feedback

**Competency Description**  
Provides relevant feedback to developers and teams who design technological tools.

**Components**

- Clearly documents bugs or improvements to suggest.  
- Communicates any issues encountered and how they affect the work.

**Instructions**

- Clear, constructive feedback to developers helps optimize and continually improve language technology tools.

---

# Interpersonal Competencies

## Responsibility

**Competency Description**  
Effectively manages commitments and keeps one’s word.

**Components**

- Meets deadlines and objectives.  
- Adjusts workload to avoid burnout.  
- Is accountable to colleagues and supervisors.

**Instructions**

- Being reliable and capable of managing responsibilities well builds trust and enhances service quality.

---

## Self-Awareness

**Competency Description**  
Understands personal strengths and weaknesses and takes steps to improve when working in a team.

**Components**

- Recognizes how one’s personality affects collaborative work.  
- Accepts feedback without getting upset unnecessarily.  
- Seeks to address personal weaknesses in a constructive way.

**Instructions**

- Greater self-awareness leads to better interactions and adaptability in various work contexts.

---

## Social Skills

**Competency Description**  
Communicates respectfully and constructively, and maintains good relationships within a team.

**Components**

- Actively listens to others.  
- Gives and receives criticism in a constructive manner.  
- Offers guidance and encourages personal development.  
- Recognizes achievements and addresses conflicts with tact.

**Instructions**

- Positive professional relationships are crucial for creating an environment that fosters collaboration and successful projects.

---

# Language and Culture

## Social Anthropology

**Competency Description**  
Respects cultural differences and knows how to work according to local norms.

**Components**

- Observes and learns about the customs and practices of the community.  
- Interacts with empathy and respect for cultural differences.  
- Adapts communication style to local cultural standards.

**Instructions**

- A sound cultural understanding helps ensure that communities accept the consultant’s work and remain engaged with the tools and processes.

---

## Use of Language

**Competency Description**  
Communicates clearly in the most appropriate vehicular language.

**Components**

- Expresses ideas clearly and understandably, both verbally and in writing.  
- Chooses the right language for the context to ensure comprehension by all.  
- Simplifies technical concepts when explaining to non-specialists.

**Instructions**

- Adequate proficiency in the local or vehicular language is crucial for training and assisting teams without communication barriers.

---

# Skills Required for a Consulting Session

## Participatory Approaches

**Competency Description**  
Uses methods that encourage active participation from learners or the teams being supported.

**Components**

- Arranges a discovery session to identify needs and issues.  
- Proposes ideas or solutions in a coaching-style approach.  
- Engages participants in evaluation and decision-making.

**Instructions**

- Participatory methods promote user empowerment and ensure that proposed solutions are well accepted by the teams.

---

## Implementation and Evaluation

**Competency Description**  
Guides the implementation of solutions and helps evaluate their effectiveness with the team.

**Components**

- Monitors progress after training or an intervention.  
- Checks whether results meet expectations and discusses them with the team.  
- Communicates successes and areas for improvement.

**Instructions**

- Assisting teams in applying and assessing solutions ensures genuine adoption and provides a foundation for future adjustments.

---

## Establishing a Procedure

**Competency Description**  
Sets up a clear framework for the consulting or support process.

**Components**

- Defines objectives, roles, and responsibilities at the outset.  
- Listens to the organization’s or team’s needs.  
- Outlines the steps in the consultation process, from initial diagnosis to final evaluation.

**Instructions**

- A shared, well-defined procedure makes sure everyone understands the phases and expected outcomes.

---

# Program Design and Mobilization

## Context

**Competency Description**  
Considers cultural, social, and economic factors when designing or implementing a language/technology program or project.

**Components**

- Analyzes local factors that may affect adoption of new technology tools.  
- Engages the community to ensure ownership of the project.

**Instructions**

- Designing a program that reflects local socio-linguistic realities increases its relevance and sustainability.

---

## Involvement

**Competency Description**  
Works respectfully and responsibly with the community and stakeholders.

**Components**

- Recognizes the community’s rights and ownership of its own project.  
- Participates in administrative details (intellectual property rights, licenses, etc.) as needed.  
- Maintains a positive attitude toward partners, even when disagreements arise.

**Instructions**

- A consultant’s involvement throughout all phases of a project, while respecting each partner’s role, strengthens credibility and effectiveness.

---

## Strategy

**Competency Description**  
Understands the basic principles for developing and managing a language or technology project.

**Components**

- Identifies key stages of a program (planning, implementation, evaluation).  
- Contributes constructive ideas for continuous program improvement.  
- Explains why it is important to involve a variety of disciplines.

**Instructions**

- Knowing the strategic aspects of a program helps consultants better assist teams and coordinate available resources.

---

# Relationships with Other Organizations

## Influencing and Building a Network

**Competency Description**  
Maintains respectful and constructive relationships with external partners, either to learn from them or to have a positive influence.

**Components**

- Clearly communicates the project’s values and needs in the LT field.  
- Represents local linguistic communities by highlighting their challenges and priorities.  
- Seeks potential partners or useful contacts.

**Instructions**

- Developing a network and advocating for project needs help secure support and foster productive collaborations.

---

## Relationship with Partners

**Competency Description**  
Collaborates effectively with a range of partners and represents one’s own organization professionally.

**Components**

- Explains the importance of language technology to non-specialists.  
- Respects the principles and values of other organizations.  
- Aims for mutually beneficial (win-win) solutions.

**Instructions**

- Positive partnerships can lead to new funding opportunities, technical support, and greater recognition of the work being done.

---

# Research and Documentation

## Intellectual Property

**Competency Description**  
Understands basic concepts of copyright and licensing for resources and technology tools.

**Components**

- Distinguishes between different license types (copyright, Creative Commons, etc.).  
- Adheres to copyright regulations in all projects.

**Instructions**

- Working ethically and legally in terms of intellectual property rights protects the project and encourages responsible resource sharing.

---

## Publication and Archiving

**Competency Description**  
Knows how to document and archive resources so they remain accessible and reusable.

**Components**

- Writes training or reference materials that are clear and concise.  
- Classifies and archives documents (text files, databases, etc.) in an organized manner.

**Instructions**

- Good document management capitalizes on completed work and avoids duplication of effort in future projects.

---

## Research

**Competency Description**  
Keeps abreast of new developments and contributes, at one’s own level, to improving practices or research in the field.

**Components**

- Stays informed about updates (software, methods, etc.).  
- Shares experiences or observations with the community.  
- Asks questions and actively participates in trainings, workshops, or conferences.

**Instructions**

- Staying current and exchanging best practices fosters innovation and increases the quality of services provided.

---

# Technologies Used for Consulting

## Communication and Collaboration Tools

**Competency Description**  
Effectively uses digital tools for remote training, collaboration, and file-sharing.

**Components**

- Is proficient with videoconferencing, email, and instant messaging tools.  
- Securely shares documents (cloud, version tracking) with team members.  
- Clarifies expectations and roles at the outset of any online consulting.

**Instructions**

- Making good use of communication tools saves time and improves the quality of collaborative work, both in person and remotely.

---

## Data Organization

**Competency Description**  
Manages, backs up, and protects digital data responsibly.

**Components**

- Maintains computers and keeps software updated.  
- Regularly backs up and organizes files so they are easy to find.  
- Protects data confidentiality (passwords, limited access, etc.).

**Instructions**

- Proper data management and backups ensure continuity of work and protect sensitive information.

---

# Adult Education

## Practice

**Competency Description**  
Applies methods suited to training adults in diverse contexts.

**Components**

- Identifies the specific needs of adult learners (technical level, culture, etc.).  
- Uses participatory, hands-on approaches to encourage active learning.  
- Adapts teaching style to cultural differences.

**Instructions**

- Training adults involves varying methods (demonstrations, hands-on exercises, discussions) and respecting each person’s learning pace.

---

## Theory

**Competency Description**  
Knows the main principles of adult education and can explain them simply.

**Components**

- Explains the basics of active learning (dialogue, role-plays, hands-on activities, etc.).  
- Recognizes that learners have diverse styles (auditory, visual, kinesthetic).

**Instructions**

- A solid grasp of adult education theory enhances session design and delivery, leading to better outcomes and knowledge transfer.

---

# Mentoring

## Attitudes

**Competency Description**  
Creates a positive environment that encourages mentees to grow professionally.

**Components**

- Shows genuine care, celebrates successes, and recognizes personal limits.  
- Believes in the mentee’s potential and highlights their achievements.

**Instructions**

- Attitudes of listening and encouragement are key to establishing trust and motivating mentees.

---

## Principles

**Competency Description**  
Understands the difference between formal mentoring, informal mentoring, coaching, and facilitation.

**Components**

- Explains how formal mentoring works (objectives, roles, duration).  
- Clarifies mutual expectations (mentor, mentee, supervisor) at the start.

**Instructions**

- A clear framework (objectives, boundaries, follow-up) ensures mentoring is effective and that each person knows their role.

---

## Skills

**Competency Description**  
Uses the necessary know-how to guide and support mentees.

**Components**

- Sets specific objectives with the mentee and follows up regularly.  
- Provides respectful and constructive feedback.  
- Maintains confidentiality regarding shared experiences.

**Instructions**

- Mentoring strengthens everyone’s ability to learn, to train others, and to contribute to the overall development of the team.

---

# Working in a Multicultural Environment

## Intercultural Communication

**Competency Description**  
Communicates with people from other cultures in a respectful and appropriate manner.

**Components**

- Identifies different communication styles and adapts accordingly.  
- Recognizes that leadership or conflict management can vary by culture.  
- Remains flexible and open-minded in intercultural training and mentoring.

**Instructions**

- Good intercultural communication fosters collaboration and prevents misunderstandings that could hinder teamwork.

---

## Multicultural Work

**Competency Description**  
Works effectively in teams or projects involving people of diverse cultural backgrounds.

**Components**

- Considers cultural differences when encouraging positive group dynamics.  
- Actively participates in decision-making by including all voices.  
- Respects local authorities and customs.

**Instructions**

- Understanding and valuing cultural differences contribute to a harmonious work environment and better project outcomes.
