# Module 2: Word Collection & Lexicon Development 

### Connect (5 minutes)
**Quick poll:** Have you ever used a dictionary app on your phone? What made it useful (or frustrating)?
Think about this: dictionaries don’t just list words—they help people understand meanings, pronunciation, and how words work in sentences. The same is true for language development dictionaries.
### Content (15 minutes)
Watch the video  Lexicon Development Tools
**Word Collection Tools**
**Three approaches to building word collections:**
1. Semantic domains (grouping by category: family, food, tools)
2. Comparative Africa word list and MLE 4000 word list
3. Rapid Word Collection (RWC) with community speakers
**Primary Tools:**
**WeSay** - Simple and community-friendly
- Designed for mother-tongue speakers
- Built-in semantic domain support
- Supports Africa comparative word list
- Easy data exchange with FLEx
**The Combine** - Purpose-built for Rapid Word Collection
- Brings native speakers together to gather comprehensive word lists
- Collects words, translations, audio recordings, and notes
- Easy organization and editing before export to FLEx
- Preserves language richness for future generations
**FieldWorks Language Explorer (FLEx)** - Powerful and linguistically sophisticated
- Professional-grade lexicon management
- Semantic domain support
- Full integration with grammar and text analysis
- Industry standard for serious linguistic work
**FieldWorks Lite** - Streamlined lexicon builder
- Simpler than FLEx, more powerful than WeSay
- Fast and easy to use
- Designed as WeSay and Language Forge replacement
- Companion application for FLEx projects
**Output:** A word list for phonological analysis and the foundation of a dictionary

### Challenge (10 minutes)
**Matching Exercise:**
Match each tool to the best scenario:
1. A team of 3 community members wants to quickly gather 2,000 words this month
2. A linguist needs detailed morphological analysis integrated with lexicon work
3. A literacy team wants community members to add simple definitions on their own
4. The project wants an Android app so youth can search the dictionary on their phones
5. A simplified tool is needed that’s easier than FLEx but more capable than WeSay
**Tools:**
- A. FLEx
- B. WeSay
- C. The Combine
- D. Dictionary App Builder
- E. FieldWorks Lite
**Troubleshooting Scenario:**
A team emails you: “We collected 1,500 words in WeSay, but now our linguist wants to do morphological analysis. Do we need to start over in FLEx?”
What do you tell them? (Hint: think about data exchange)
